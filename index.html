Advanced Dental Digitization: Incorporating Shape Priors and Multiview Coherence in Diffusion Models for Tooth Model Design

Paper

Abstract

Automatic design of 3D tooth model plays an important role in dental digitization. However, diffusion models in multivew editing rely on score distillation sampling (SDS) to enforce 3D awareness, which cannot model the joint image distribution of multiple views, leading to a drawback in 3D consistency. In addition to that, the collision avoidance term in NeRF fine-tuning often yields wield results owing to the lack of shape prior with regard to human tooth. Motivated by the energy-based distribution modeling and diffusion model-based shape denoising, we improve the iterative dataset update (IDU) as follows: 1) A multiview score distillation function (MSDS) is derived in the multiview editing stage by incorporating the inter-view coherence compatible function; 2) The defective tooth is renovated by adding shape prior distilled by a diffusion model to increase the realism in the NeRF fine-tuning stage. Experimental results on two tooth design datasets show that our approach improves the multiview consistency and realism of the generated tooth by a margin when it is compared with prevailing approaches.
